Location Analysis

Metropolitan Dominance

Metropolitan areas account for the overwhelming majority of infractions across all jurisdictions. This concentration reflects higher traffic volumes, more extensive camera infrastructure, and focused enforcement in urban centers.

  • Total metro infractions: ~167,000 (73%)
  • Average metro rate: 3x regional areas
  • Highest density: Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane

Regional Patterns

Regional areas show moderate infraction rates, with significant variation based on tourism, major highways, and local enforcement priorities. Holiday destinations show seasonal spikes that align with tourist periods.

  • Total regional infractions: ~48,000 (21%)
  • Highway corridors show highest rates
  • Tourist areas peak during holidays

Remote Area Challenges

Remote areas represent only 6% of total infractions but show concerning patterns. Higher severity offenses, longer response times, and limited enforcement capacity create unique challenges for road safety in these regions.

  • Total remote infractions: ~13,000 (6%)
  • Higher average severity (+23%)
  • Limited camera infrastructure

Hotspot Identification

Specific locations consistently emerge as high-infraction zones. These hotspots often correlate with complex road infrastructure, high traffic volumes, confusing signage, or strategic enforcement locations.

  • Top 10 locations: 35% of all infractions
  • Motorway corridors dominate hotspots
  • School zones show enforcement peaks
