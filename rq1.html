Highest Monthly Fines

Victoria consistently records the highest monthly speeding fines, with peaks exceeding 102,000 fines in single months.

Seasonal Variation

Most jurisdictions show increased enforcement during March-May and August-November periods.

Regional Differences

Larger states (NSW, VIC, QLD) show significantly higher absolute numbers, while territories (ACT, NT) maintain lower but consistent patterns.

Jurisdiction Comparison Analysis

Victoria's Leading Position

Victoria consistently leads all jurisdictions in monthly speeding fines throughout 2024, reflecting both its high population density and extensive automated enforcement infrastructure.

  • Peak month: March with 102,386 fines
  • Average monthly: ~87,000 fines
  • Consistent enforcement year-round

NSW and QLD Patterns

New South Wales and Queensland follow similar monthly trends with moderate seasonal variation. Both states show increased enforcement during mid-year and spring periods.

  • NSW average: ~75,000 monthly fines
  • QLD average: ~52,000 monthly fines
  • Similar seasonal patterns observed

Territory Stability

ACT and NT demonstrate more stable monthly patterns with less variation. Their smaller populations and focused enforcement areas result in consistent monthly figures.

  • ACT: 5,000-7,500 monthly range
  • NT: Similar low-volume consistency
  • Urban concentration effect visible

Seasonal Insights

Most jurisdictions show reduced fines during winter months (June-July) and holiday periods (December-January), likely reflecting reduced travel and enforcement resources during these times.

  • Winter dip: 10-15% reduction in June-July
  • Holiday season: Lower December volumes
  • Spring peak: October-November increases
