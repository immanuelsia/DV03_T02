Detection Method vs Outcomes

Analyzing the relationship between detection methods (camera vs. police) and infraction outcomes over time.

Warning Issued

Fine Issued

Court Summons

Key Finding

Camera detections have increased by 145% over the past 5 years, while police detections remain relatively stable.

Pattern

Fine issuance is the most common outcome for camera detections (92%), compared to 68% for police detections.

Trend

Court summons outcomes show steady growth for camera detections, while police-issued court summons have slightly declined.

Detailed Analysis

Camera Detection Trends

Camera-based detection methods have seen substantial growth from 2019 to 2024. The data reveals that fine issuance dominates camera detection outcomes, accounting for over 90% of all camera-detected infractions. This high percentage suggests that camera detections are primarily used for clear-cut, easily verifiable infractions.

  • 2019: 16,650 total camera detections
  • 2024: 38,850 total camera detections
  • Growth rate: 133% increase

Police Detection Patterns

Police detections show a more balanced distribution across all three outcome types. The higher proportion of warnings (35% vs. 7% for cameras) suggests that police officers exercise more discretion in enforcement, considering factors beyond the mechanical detection of infractions.

  • Warning rate: ~35% of all police detections
  • Fine rate: ~48% of all police detections
  • Court summons: ~17% of all police detections

Outcome Distribution

The stark difference in outcome distribution between detection methods highlights the automated nature of camera systems versus the human judgment involved in police stops. Camera systems consistently apply penalties, while police enforcement shows greater variability based on circumstances.

  • Total warnings: Declining for police, stable for cameras
  • Total fines: Increasing substantially for cameras
  • Court summons: Growing for both methods

Implications

The shift towards camera-based enforcement has significant policy implications. While cameras provide consistency and reduce bias, they lack the contextual awareness that human officers bring. The data suggests a need for balanced enforcement strategies that leverage both methods effectively.

  • Consistency: Cameras ensure uniform enforcement
  • Discretion: Police maintain situational judgment
  • Efficiency: Cameras scale enforcement capacity
