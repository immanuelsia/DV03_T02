Jurisdiction Comparison

Major State Analysis

NSW, Victoria, and Queensland, as the three most populous states, show distinct enforcement patterns. Victoria's higher per-capita rate suggests more intensive enforcement, while Queensland shows a balanced mix of detection methods.

  • NSW: 77,000 total infractions
  • VIC: 69,500 total infractions
  • QLD: 51,800 total infractions

Territory Differences

ACT and NT show interesting contrasts. ACT's high camera adoption reflects urban density and infrastructure investment, while NT's lower detection rates may relate to sparse population distribution and vast distances.

  • ACT: 84% camera detection
  • NT: Higher proportion of police detection
  • Geographic challenges affect enforcement

Camera vs Police Distribution

Camera adoption varies significantly by jurisdiction, with larger states generally showing higher automation rates. This reflects both infrastructure investment capacity and policy decisions about enforcement approaches.

  • High automation: NSW, VIC, ACT (>80%)
  • Moderate: QLD, WA, SA (70-80%)
  • Lower automation: TAS, NT (<70%)

Policy Implications

The variation in detection rates and methods suggests different policy priorities across jurisdictions. Higher per-capita enforcement doesn't necessarily correlate with better road safety outcomes, indicating the need for holistic evaluation.

  • Enforcement intensity varies 4x across states
  • Camera adoption ranges from 63% to 85%
  • Population density influences method choice
