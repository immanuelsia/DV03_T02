Age Groups Adjusted for License Holders

Analyzing infraction rates per 10,000 license holders across different age demographics.

Highest Risk Group

Drivers aged 17-24 show the highest infraction rate at 284 per 10,000 license holders.

Safest Group

Drivers aged 55-64 demonstrate the lowest infraction rate, with only 67 per 10,000 license holders.

Trend

Infraction rates show a steady declining trend with increasing age, with a slight uptick in the 75+ category.

Demographic Analysis

üë• Young Drivers (17-24)

Young drivers show dramatically higher infraction rates even when adjusted for the number of license holders. This group's rate is 4.2x higher than the safest age group, indicating significant risk factors including inexperience, risk-taking behavior, and lifestyle patterns.

  • Rate: 284 per 10,000 license holders
  • Total infractions: 35,500
  • Most common: Speeding and mobile phone use

üöó Young Adults (25-34)

The 25-34 age group shows elevated but declining infraction rates compared to teenagers. This transition period reflects increasing driving experience and potentially more stable lifestyle patterns, though rates remain notably above middle-aged drivers.

  • Rate: 230 per 10,000 license holders
  • Total infractions: 48,300
  • 19% reduction from 17-24 group

üëî Middle-Aged Drivers (35-54)

Middle-aged drivers demonstrate significantly lower infraction rates, reflecting decades of driving experience, established routines, and potentially different time pressures. This group represents the baseline for safe driving behavior.

  • 35-44: 180 per 10,000 license holders
  • 45-54: 142 per 10,000 license holders
  • Stable, responsible driving patterns

üë¥ Older Drivers (55+)

Older drivers show the lowest infraction rates across most age brackets, with the 55-64 group demonstrating exceptional compliance. The slight increase in the 75+ category may reflect changing abilities rather than intentional violations.

  • 55-64: 67 per 10,000 (lowest rate)
  • 65-74: 70 per 10,000
  • 75+: 90 per 10,000 (slight increase)

Targeted Education

The dramatic difference between young and experienced drivers suggests significant opportunity for targeted education programs. Enhanced graduated licensing programs and mandatory awareness training could help reduce the young driver infraction rate.

üìä

Risk-Based Enforcement

Understanding age-based risk profiles enables more effective resource allocation. Focusing enforcement and education on high-risk demographics and locations frequented by younger drivers could yield better safety outcomes.

Behavioral Change

The clear improvement with age demonstrates that driver behavior can and does change. Programs that accelerate this maturation process through peer mentoring or advanced training could reduce infractions in younger cohorts.
